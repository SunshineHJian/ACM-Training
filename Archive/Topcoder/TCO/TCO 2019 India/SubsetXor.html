Problem Statement

    

Alice has a list (A) containing some positive integers. She asks Bob to pick any non-empty subset of numbers from the list and compute its XOR.

Bob writes down the computed value in a notebook. She asks him to pick subsets repeatedly until all subsets of the list are chosen at least once. Bob being intelligent, won't pick a previously chosen subset again.

Find the smallest positive number that Bob cannot write in his notebook.

Definition

    
Class:SubsetXor
Method:computeSmallest
Parameters:vector<long long>
Returns:long long
Method signature:long long computeSmallest(vector<long long> A)
(be sure your method is public)

Limits

    
Time limit (s):2.000
Memory limit (MB):256

Notes

-The XOR of an empty subset is defined to be 0 and the XOR of a subset with exactly 1 element is the number itself.

Constraints

-The length of A will be between 1 and 1000, inclusive.
-Each number in A will be between 1 (inclusive) and 262 (exclusive).
-It is guaranteed that the answer would be between 1 (inclusive) and 263 (exclusive).

Examples

0)
    
{2000, 300, 46}
Returns: 1
Bob would select the following subsets and write the corresponding XOR values: 
{2000} => 2000
{300} => 300
{46} => 46
{2000, 300} => 2000 ^ 300 = 1788
{300, 46} => 300 ^ 46 = 258
{2000, 46} => 2000 ^ 46 = 2046
{2000, 300, 46} => 2000 ^ 300 ^ 46 = 1746
The smallest positive number that Bob won't write in his notebook is 1.
1)
    
{1, 2}
Returns: 4
Bob would write the XORs of subsets {1}, {2} and {1, 2} which are 1, 2 and 3 respectively. The smallest number that he won't write in his notebook is 4.
